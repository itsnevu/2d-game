1/
WILDER

A 2D survival MMO set in a world that already ended.

No tutorial. No safe zone. You wash up by a shipwreck with nothing — surrounded by the ruins of a civilization that's already gone.

Here's what it actually is 🧵↓

2/
The world ended. Then it kept going.

You arrive late — a survivor walking over the wreckage of everyone who came before. The stations still run. The lights still flicker. But the people are long gone.

You're not building a new world. You're surviving inside a dead one.

3/
Three civilizations came first.

🔹 The Aleut — the first to live here.
🔹 The Admiralty Directorate — machine-age power whose stations still hum in the dark.
🔹 The Modern Survivors — you.

The whole map is a history book you're walking across.

4/
One island. One world. Real players.

No shards, no instances. Up to 50 survivors share a single persistent world in real time. That figure on the horizon is another player — and they can see you too.

You'll cross paths whether you want to or not.

5/
Survival is a full simulation, not a health bar.

Hunger, thirst, temperature, getting wet — every stat is its own system pushing toward the same ending. Chop, mine, forage, scavenge. The world gives you nothing for free.

6/
Build a home — and defend it.

A deep crafting tree (with a real craft queue), walls, doors, shelters, storage, turrets and drones to guard it. But bases decay — a home is something you maintain, not just place.

7/
Then build a life.

Cook over campfires and the broth pot 🍲, farm the land, collect rain, fish the coast, snorkel the tide pools. Slowly you turn a death-trap into a homestead.

8/
You are not the top of the food chain.

Melee, ranged, grenades, explosives — armor that wears down with every fight. Wild animals roam, and the old factions are still out there: the Shardkin, the Shorebound, the Drowned Watch. None welcome you.

9/
A dead world that still has a voice.

ECHO, an AI oracle from the old systems, guides you in the dark. Earn XP, watch your level rise, take quests, uncover monuments and memory fragments. Pick from 8 survivors to wash ashore.

10/
Watch for free. Survive with a wallet.

Drop in as a spectator — no login, no commitment. When you're ready to actually live (and die) in it, connect a wallet and step in.

The world is already dead. How long do you last?
